# Design: AI Product Ad Generator

Generated by /office-hours on 2026-04-30

Status: APPROVED
Mode: Startup

## Problem Statement

We are replacing the FitMirror virtual try-on direction with a narrower ecommerce creative tool:

> Help solo cross-border ecommerce sellers turn one product image into English ad creative packages for TikTok, Reels, and Meta.

The target user is not an enterprise brand or Amazon power seller. The target user is an individual ecommerce operator who runs a small store, lacks creative staff, and needs usable ad assets quickly.

The first product should not be framed as a generic AI video platform. The buyer is paying for usable ad creative, not model access.

## Demand Evidence

Direct customer evidence is not yet available. The founder does not currently have users, revenue, or named sellers who have asked for this.

External market signals support the direction:

- Amazon Ads has launched AI Video Generator for advertisers, creating multi-scene video ads from product images, videos, or Amazon product detail pages within minutes.
- Wyzowl 2026 video marketing data reports that businesses broadly use video marketing, marketers are adopting AI video tools, and consumers prefer short product videos when learning about products.
- Existing tools such as Revid, VibePeak, Scenith, and AdStellar already sell variations of product-to-video or ecommerce ad generation.

This means the market exists, but it also means a generic "upload product image, generate video" product is already crowded. The first wedge must be narrower and more workflow-specific.

## Status Quo

The assumed current workflow for target users:

- Use Canva, CapCut, Jianying, freelancers, UGC creators, or manual phone-shot videos.
- Reuse static product images because producing video takes too much time.
- Write ad copy manually or copy competitor ads.
- Test too few creative angles because each new video takes effort.
- Lack a repeatable workflow for turning one product into multiple ad concepts.

The real competitor is not only other AI video tools. It is the seller doing nothing, posting static images, or spending hours with basic design and editing tools.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

Target user:

- Solo or very small cross-border ecommerce seller.
- Has product photos and basic product descriptions.
- Wants English ads for TikTok, Instagram Reels, Facebook, or Meta placements.
- Does not have a creative team.
- Needs fast, low-cost ad assets, not a full advertising platform.

First wedge:

> Product image to English 9:16 ad creative package.

Input:

- One product image.
- Product name.
- Three key selling points.
- Optional target audience.
- Optional promotion or offer.

Output:

- Three ad angles: pain-point, benefit, urgency/promotion.
- English hook, body copy, CTA, and caption for each angle.
- One generated vertical MP4, 10-15 seconds, based on the best angle.
- Static ad cover image or thumbnail direction.
- Download action for generated media.

## Constraints

- Timebox: 5 days.
- Product stage: pre-product, no users, no revenue.
- Existing repo has only a README and no application code.
- Do not self-train or fine-tune a model in the first version.
- Use third-party model/API providers for generation.
- Treat model ownership as a future option, not the first milestone.
- Optimize for a complete user-visible flow, not a complete SaaS backend.

## Premises

1. The first version is an ecommerce ad creative generator, not a broad AI video platform.
2. The first version uses third-party models/APIs and reserves self-owned models for later.
3. The first version only supports English 9:16 product ads.
4. The smallest sellable unit is an ad creative package.
5. The first release validates demand. It does not prove the full technical platform.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: 5-Day Demo-First MVP + Creative Engine

Build a real web tool with a complete upload-to-download flow. The product generates three English ad concepts and one 9:16 MP4. The differentiation comes from the creative package workflow, not from claiming a better base model.

Effort: Medium
Risk: Medium

Pros:

- Most likely to ship in 5 days.
- Gives users a complete loop: upload product, generate ad, download result.
- Leaves room to add credits, subscriptions, and multi-model support later.

Cons:

- Video quality depends on third-party APIs.
- Differentiation must come from templates, workflow, and ad logic.
- Requires immediate user outreach to avoid becoming only a polished demo.

### Approach B: Full SaaS MVP

Build login, credits, subscriptions, generation history, R2 storage, webhooks, failure refunds, and provider abstraction from day one.

Effort: Large
Risk: High

Pros:

- Best long-term SaaS architecture.
- Can accept real payment and track generation cost.
- Cleaner foundation for multi-model aggregation.

Cons:

- Too much for 5 days before demand is validated.
- Payment, webhook, storage, and refund paths can consume the whole sprint.
- The user-facing creative experience may become weak.

### Approach C: Creative Engine Prototype

Focus on ad strategy first. Analyze product input, produce hooks, angles, scripts, thumbnails, and creative recommendations, then generate one video from the strongest angle.

Effort: Medium to Large
Risk: Medium to High

Pros:

- More differentiated than a generic AI video generator.
- Matches the user's real gap: they lack creative direction, not just rendering.
- Can evolve into templates, scoring, and eventually proprietary model/data loops.

Cons:

- If the creative logic is weak, the product feels like generic AI copywriting.
- Video output may feel secondary.
- Scope can easily expand beyond 5 days.

## Recommended Approach

Choose Approach A, with the strongest part of Approach C embedded:

> Ship the 5-day demo-first MVP, but make the core object an "ad creative package" instead of a raw video generation task.

The first version should feel like:

1. "Upload your product."
2. "Tell us what makes it sell."
3. "Get three ad angles."
4. "Generate one ready-to-post 9:16 video."
5. "Download the ad package."

This is narrow enough to build and differentiated enough to avoid being another generic model wrapper.

## Product Flow

### Landing / Generator

The first screen is the product. No long marketing hero.

Required fields:

- Product image upload.
- Product name.
- Product category.
- Three selling points.
- Target audience.
- Offer or discount.

Optional controls:

- Tone: direct response, premium, playful, problem-solution.
- Platform: TikTok/Reels/Meta.
- Ad angle preference: auto, pain, benefit, urgency.

### Creative Package Preview

Before video generation, show three generated concepts:

1. Pain-point angle.
2. Benefit angle.
3. Urgency/promotion angle.

Each concept includes:

- Hook.
- 3-scene storyboard.
- On-screen text.
- Caption.
- CTA.

User selects one angle or lets the system choose.

### Video Generation

Generate one 9:16 video for the selected angle.

First version can accept provider limitations. The important product behavior:

- Show queued / generating / failed / complete states.
- Never silently fail.
- Preserve the creative package even if video generation fails.
- Let the user retry with the same package.

### Result

Result page includes:

- Generated MP4 preview.
- Download MP4.
- Copy caption.
- Copy CTA.
- Regenerate from same product.
- Email capture or lightweight login prompt.

## Five-Day Build Plan

### Day 1: Product Reset + App Skeleton

- Rename positioning away from FitMirror.
- Create product spec and route map.
- Scaffold Next.js app.
- Build generator form UI.
- Choose placeholder brand name.

### Day 2: Creative Engine

- Implement structured prompt templates for three ad angles.
- Generate JSON creative package with hook, scenes, on-screen text, caption, CTA.
- Add validation and fallback if the model returns malformed output.
- Show creative package preview before video generation.

### Day 3: Image/Video Generation

- Connect one stable image/video provider.
- Generate one 9:16 MP4 from selected creative angle.
- Add generation states and retry.
- Store local or temporary result URLs for MVP.

### Day 4: Result Experience + SEO Pages

- Build result/download page.
- Add copy-to-clipboard for captions and CTA.
- Add five SEO landing pages:
  - `/ai-product-video-generator`
  - `/shopify-product-video-generator`
  - `/tiktok-product-video-generator`
  - `/ai-video-ad-generator`
  - `/product-image-to-video`
- Add privacy, terms, contact placeholders.

### Day 5: Polish + Validation Launch

- Improve templates for 3-5 common categories.
- Add sample products and sample outputs.
- Add analytics events:
  - product form started
  - creative package generated
  - video generation started
  - video completed
  - download clicked
  - email captured
- Publish the site.
- Reach out to 20 sellers or communities with sample outputs.

## Success Criteria

Five-day product success:

- A real product image can produce a complete creative package.
- At least one 9:16 video can be generated and downloaded.
- The result page gives a seller usable copy and CTA even if the video is imperfect.
- At least five SEO pages exist and link into the generator.
- At least one analytics path records the conversion funnel.

Market validation success:

- 20 target users see the product or sample outputs.
- 5 upload real product images or request a generation.
- 2 say they would pay or ask about pricing.
- At least 1 asks for another variation, batch generation, or a specific platform style.

Do not count compliments as demand. Count usage, repeated requests, willingness to pay, or seller-provided product assets.

Product:

- API provider credentials.
- Basic sample products for demos.

Technical:

- Next.js app scaffold.
- LLM provider for creative package generation.
- Image/video provider for MP4 generation.

Business:

- Pricing hypothesis.
- First five ecommerce categories to support.

## The Assignment

Before or during implementation, collect 20 real product pages from small cross-border sellers and manually create one sample ad angle for each.

The goal is not research theater. The goal is to learn which product categories produce compelling outputs and which sellers react strongly enough to share a real product image.
